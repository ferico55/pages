🏆 Best Overall Value for Money

Toyota Agya 1.2L G CVT (Rp 197 jt) for petrol. BYD M6 Standard (Rp 383 jt) for EV. The Agya gives you Toyota reliability, proven fuel efficiency, and the widest service network for the least money. The M6 gives you a 7-seater electric MPV at a price that simply has no equal in Indonesia — the value proposition is extraordinary.

🔧 Best Reliability & Durability

Toyota Agya / Daihatsu Ayla for petrol — Japanese reliability with 10+ years of proven track record in Indonesia, cheap spare parts everywhere. For EV, Hyundai Kona Electric wins clearly — built locally in Bojongmangu, Hyundai's EV battery has the best safety record in Asia, and the 4-year warranty + free service package is best-in-class.

🍎 Apple CarPlay Availability

Daihatsu Sirion CVT is the only petrol option with CarPlay on all variants. For EVs, all BYD models and Kona Electric include CarPlay. If CarPlay matters on a tight budget, consider BYD Atto 1 Premium (Rp 235 jt) — the most affordable CarPlay-equipped car in this list. Note: Toyota Agya and Honda Brio Satya only offer CarPlay on their top trims (GR Sport / RS CVT).

☀️ Sunroof Options

No petrol cars in this comparison come with a sunroof. For EVs: BYD Atto 3 Advanced Plus (Rp 415 jt) is the most affordable option with a panoramic sunroof. BYD M6 Superior (Rp 423 jt) also includes it. The Hyundai Kona Electric only adds a sunroof on the Signature and N Line variants (Rp 649 jt+). If sunroof is important, the BYD Atto 3 Advanced Plus offers the best sunroof-per-rupiah.

📍 For Sunter, Jakarta Utara

Nearest dealer concentrations are around Kelapa Gading and Sunter area. Toyota Auto2000 and Honda have multiple branches nearby — strong advantage for the Agya/Brio if after-sales convenience matters. For BYD, the nearest dealers are around Kelapa Gading and Pluit. Hyundai has a branch in Kelapa Gading as well. EV charging infra in North Jakarta is growing but still less dense than South — worth considering for daily charging habits.
